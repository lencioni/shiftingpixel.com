Christ Chapel Pews: Near

An old ornament by Joe Lencioni

January 4, 2006 by Joe Lencioni
A blue, glass ornament on my Christmas tree ƒ/5.6, 8 s, 100 ISO

Similar photos

While it is similar to yesterday’s shot of my stocking ornament, this one is closer to the shot I was actually going for. I do like it quite a bit, but I really wanted to showcase the decay in these ornaments.

You see, I got like four boxes of ornaments from my parents that were all covered with dust and the metallic colored foil is all cracked and peeling. I tried cleaning as much of the dust off as I could, but even more foil came off with each wipe. But, since I don’t have much of a selection of Christmas ornaments yet, I put up the dust-coated ornaments with all of their glorious missing foil.

Unfortunately, this shot doesn’t exactly showcase the decay. I think that it mostly has to do with the lighting. I think that that good photography roughly boils down to about 50% lighting, 30% composition, and 20% technical skill. So perhaps I will have another run at these Christmas bulbs after I get them off the dang tree.

A fun little bit of trivia: reflected in this particular shot is a giant 20″x30″ framed print of this photograph that’s hanging on my kitchen wall. And, speaking of prints, a quick poll: would you be interested in purchasing prints of my photographs? I am thinking about setting up a process to easily facilitate orders but might not want to bother if it wouldn’t be worth the time. Let me know your thoughts.
